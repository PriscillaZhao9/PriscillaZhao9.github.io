--- layout: page title: "About Me" --- Hiiii! My name is Priscilla(Pu) Zhao (Chinese: 赵普). I am a first year Education Data Science Master student at Stanford Graduate School of Education working with Professor Jelena Obradović in the Spark Lab and Professor Mark Warschauer in the Digital Learning Lab. My research mainly focuses on early childhood development, specifically using artificial intelligence systems to mimic and promote children’s curiosity and designing technologies to support language and literacy development. I am also interested in children's psychology and developing effective interventions to support children’s functioning in relationships, socioemotional development, well-being, and academic performances.

Before joining Stanford, I got my bachelor's degree in Pure Math and Communication & Media Studies from the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. I worked on Michigan early childhood policy analysis in Education Policy Initiative(PI: Brian Jacob and Gloria Yeomans-Maldonado), gameful learning analysis of GradeCraft (PI: Barry Fishman) and Google image search algorithm biases analysis in Center for Ethics, Society and Computing (PI: Christian Sandvig).
